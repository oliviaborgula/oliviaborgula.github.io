Graphics and Projects



JavaScript/scrolly-telling — D.C. walkability

I used JavaScript to shows how different parts of D.C. compare in terms of environmental factors like access to bike lanes, tree canopy coverage and more.

R — Baltimore voter analysis

Correlation between voter turnout rate and inequality in Baltimore City. I used Tabula to extract precinct-level data from Baltimore City PDFs, cleaned and analyzed the dataset in R and visualized it in Flourish and Datawrapper.

Adobe Illustrator — Shrinking kelp forests

Data visualizations and graphics showing the shrinking California kelp forests. I used a combination of mapping in Python, Flourish data visualizations and illustrations to tell this story, putting it all together in Illustrator.

Adobe Illustrator — MMR vaccination rate

This visualization was published in Capital News Service along with this story about an outbreak of measles in Maryland. I used CDC data for measles, mumps and rubella vaccination rates around the country and visualized it in Adobe Illustrator.

Adobe Illustrator — Swan Lake spins

I made a dataset based on the average number of rotations five different prima ballerinas make in the famous Swan Lake coda and visualized it with help from Photoshop and Illustrator.

Adobe Illustrator — Where does plastic go after it's thrown out?

I uploaded data from Our World in Data to Flourish and made a Sankey diagram, then uploaded the SVG to Illustrator and added annotations and the other design elements.

Adobe Illustrator — Death row around the U.S.

For this visualization, I used a map to show which states have disproportionately more people on death row and a waffle chart to show that Black men are overrepresented on Alabama's death row.

Adobe Illustrator — Iris features

I drew these iris illustrations to show variations in sepal and petal lengths, based on the famous Ronald Fisher iris dataset.
