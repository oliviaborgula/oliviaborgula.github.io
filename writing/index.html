  • Pride-Related Promo Searches Decline as Brands Scale Back
    July 14, 2025 | Advertising Specialty Institute

    ESP is a database where suppliers and distributors of promotional products can search for bulk items. I analyzed years worth of data using R and found that keywords related to LGBTQ+ and Pride-related items have declined since 2023, aligning with a broader corporate pullback from outward support of Pride-month events. I spoke with industry professionals as well as experts and LGBTQ+ rights activists for this story.

  • Egg prices hit record high amid bird flu outbreak
    Feb. 20, 2025 | Capital News Service

    I analyzed data from the Bureau of Labor Statistics about egg prices to localize a national issue stemming from avian influenza. This story was picked up by dozens of local news outlets, including the Baltimore Banner and Maryland Matters.

  • Nearly One-Third of Top Promo Firms Release Annual Sustainability Reports
    Aug. 6, 2025 | Advertising Specialty Institute

    I used Python to scrape news articles under ASI's sustainability tab to determine how many of the top 40 suppliers and distributors of promotional products release annual sustainability reports. I found that close to one third of all top promo firms release these types of reports, and that a majority only started releasing them in 2022, which reflects a growing trend of corporate ESG and sustainability transparency.

    May 9, 2025 | Capital News Service

    Recent federal and state legislation has pushed back on Maryland's electric vehicle expansion. I looked into a decade worth of data about Maryland's existing electric vehicle infrastructure to contextualize the impact of these policy actions.

  • Maryland reported 3 measles cases in March. Here’s what you need to know.

    Amid three reports of measles cases in Maryland, I looked into state vaccination data and spoke with experts about the significance of these cases.

    Aug. 25, 2025 | Advertising Specialty Institute

    Overall apparel import data shows rising numbers this year for top exporters in North and Central America, including Mexico, Honduras and Nicaragua – which in general have lower tariff rates – and more varied levels for top Asian exporters.

  • Maryland federal contractor layoffs increase as Trump slashes federal spending
    April 15, 2025 | Capital News Service

    More than 3,000 Maryland employees at companies that contract with the federal government received layoff notifications this year, according to a Maryland database. These layoffs will have major impacts on the state’s economy, particularly hardest-hit counties like Montgomery County, Baltimore City and Prince George's County.

  • Art in the Age of Algorithms: The Promise & Pitfalls of AI Design
    Aug. 18, 2025 | Advertising Specialty Institute

    I interviewed five people who use AI-generated artwork or design to support their work and looked into the potential and pitfalls of the technology in the art industry. Though fewer than a quarter of promo pros are using generative AI for artwork or video generation, expect that number to grow as deep learning models become more sophisticated. Still, many warn against the technology’s practical limitations and ethical issues.
